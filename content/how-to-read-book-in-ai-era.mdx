# How to Read a Book in the AI Era

## Before You Read a Book

> Inspired by [ðŸ“ Notes on How to Read a Book](https://chatgpt.com/canvas/shared/6981d2b9bc688191a91338bd413a4894)

Reading in the AI era is no longer a linear, solitary activity. With abundant computational assistance, the limiting factor is not access to information but the **quality of questions, focus, and synthesis**. Preparation before reading is therefore critical.

## Meta Considerations

* Formulate **explicit questions** before reading. High-quality questions function as *polar stars*: they guide attention, shape interpretation, and anchor new knowledge to existing mental models.
* [AI] Use AI to preview the book:
  * Ask what core problems the book addresses.
  * If applying a **comparative methodology**, ask which themes, disciplines, or adjacent works are most relevant.
* Be selective. Only a tiny fraction of books (e.g., 1 in 1,000 or 1 in 10,000) materially improve reasoning ability and conceptual depth. Identify those early.

## Analytical Reading

**Question:** What does the book actually say, in detail?

* Interpret the authorâ€™s key terms and establish shared definitions.
* Extract the main propositions from the most important passages.
* Reconstruct the arguments:
  * premises
  * causal relationships
  * conclusions
* Identify:
  * Problems the author claims to solve
  * Problems left unresolved
  * Limitations explicitly acknowledged by the author

[AI] AI can support this phase by acting as a **thinking partner**, not a replacement for judgment. Consider maintaining a structured workspace (e.g., a notebook system) that integrates:

* highlighted excerpts
* personal annotations
* counterexamples
* reflections drawn from your own experience

This enables iterative co-thinking between you and the model.

## Comparative Reading

> Synthesize multiple perspectives to produce original and valuable insight.

Comparative reading treats a **theme**, not a book, as the unit of analysis. You read across authors and sources, deliberately breaking free from any single intellectual framework.

* Define a clear and narrowly scoped research theme.
* Build an experimental bibliography using:
  * references from the primary book
  * expert recommendations
  * indexes, citations, and AI-assisted discovery
* Skim all candidate materials to:
  * select the most relevant sources
  * refine and stabilize your thematic framework

[AI] Use AI to accelerate discovery and context-building, but reserve synthesis and judgment for yourself. The value of comparative reading lies in constructing a **personal, reasoned viewpoint** that no single source could provide.
